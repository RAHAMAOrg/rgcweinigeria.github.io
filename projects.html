Our Approach

Community-Driven

We listen closely, work hand in hand with community leaders, and tailor our programs to meet specific needs of adolescents, women, children, and other vulnerable groups.

Data-Informed

We harness data to inform decisions, advocate for policy change, and continuously improve service delivery through technology and strategic partnerships.

Partnership-Focused

We collaborate with PEPFAR, Unitaid, Médecins Du Monde, EU, and others to scale our reach and deepen our impact across Nigeria.

Active Projects

Active

Girls' Education and Protection Program (GEPP)

Supported by: Girls Opportunity Alliance, Obama Foundation Location: Kano State

The Girls' Education and Protection Project aims to improve access to quality education and strengthen protection systems for adolescent girls across Kano State. Through community-based assessments, stakeholder engagement, school support, and multi-sectoral collaboration, GEPP addresses barriers that keep girls out of school—from socio-cultural norms to safety, health, and economic challenges.

Impact: Creating safer learning environments, increasing enrollment and retention, and empowering girls to thrive and reach their full potential.

Active

AYP Project

Supported by: PEPFAR Duration: 12 months Location: Kano State

This initiative transforms HIV service delivery for Adolescents and Young People (AYP) in Kano State. We enhance community-led monitoring and boost engagement in virtual spaces to ensure young people have better access to quality HIV services, empowering them with vital knowledge about prevention, testing, treatment, and care.

Impact: Strengthening community-led monitoring through innovative technology and leveraging data to drive evidence-based decision-making for improved HIV programs.

Active

Community Health Promotion and Referral Support Project

Funded by: Médecins Du Monde / EU Civil Protection Duration: June 2025 – January 2026 (8 months) Location: Zango LGA, Katsina State

This project improves public health outcomes and strengthens health system responsiveness in Zango Local Government Area. Focusing on Yardaji and Rogogo wards, we target pregnant and lactating women, elderly people, and children under five to enhance health literacy and increase uptake of essential health services.

Impact: Building resilient communities where everyone has access to quality, people-centered healthcare through empowered community structures and effective feedback mechanisms.

Recently Completed

TPT Project

Funded by: Unitaid through Treatment Action Group Duration: May 2024 – April 2025 (11 months) Location: Kano State

Building community demand for 1HP TB prevention regimen among high-risk populations. This initiative strengthened tuberculosis prevention efforts by building strong community demand and assessing the acceptability of shortened Tuberculosis Preventive Therapy (TPT) among all eligible populations.

Impact: Leveraged existing community structures and healthcare services to ensure preventive treatment is accessible and embraced, contributing to reduced TB incidence across Kano State.
